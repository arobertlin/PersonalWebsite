About Me

I'm graduating with a Princeton Computer Science degree Spring 2021. While I have the classical OOP skill set in languages like Java and C, I have the most professional experience in Python for web backend, data science, instrument interfacing, and machine learning.

Dallas born and raised, I acclimated to the cold Northeast by walking on the Varsity Water Polo Team and spending much of my time in a heated pool. I'll use just about any excuse to get outdoors, but some of my favorites are hiking, biking, fishing, and kayaking.

Work Experience

Microsoft Hololens

I worked on the laser display team of the Hololens. I ran a bring-up platform, collected, and analyzed laser color data to assist in the color calibration control loop of the display. In the process I wrote drivers for optical photodiodes and oscilloscope, firmware to control FPGA emulations of designated silicon, and python scripts to control the laser lab setup.




Sea Machines Robotics

I worked on the autonomy team for Sea Machines, which develops Level 4 autonomy systems for marine vessels. I developed an autonomous behavior so the boat would change speeds in shallow water according to the depth sensor readings. I also contributed to an open-source project for interfacing with a gps and built a socket-based connection to seamlessly pull in computer vision data from our IP camera.

Projects

Weight-Controlled Electric Skateboard

Electric Skateboards provide transportation and entertainment, but are simply too difficult to control using a remote. I developed a new remoteless-control system that detects the rider's center of mass and adjusts the speed based on body position. Using classic bathroom-scale sensors and heuristics, I developed a working prototype of a hands-free electric skateboard.

Shift Coverage Mechanism

An email-based shift coverage system designed for 10 staff members just didn't work with almost 100. But the last thing the staff wanted was another app to keep track of. I listened to the input of the staff of Princeton's Dillon Gymnasium and designed an email-based system with a cloud-based mechanism to optimize for fairness and minimize annoying emails. I prove my system strictly dominates the current system and implemented it using Google Cloud.
